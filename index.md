## About Me

I plan to graduate in December 2022 with a post-bachelorette in Computer Science from Fort Hays State University. I hold a BS in Chemical Engineering from University of Nevada, Reno. Six months after graduating, I was injured in a diving accident. I am paralyzed below the tops of my shoulders. I control my computer with my mouth, using a Quadstick, along with some voice control and dictation software. This is the reason I am pursuing research in Computer science. I hope to contribute to the assistive technology industry and tear down some barriers that are keeping some people from being independent. I plan to start a masters in computer science in Spring 2023.

## About My Mentor

Dr. Vinod Namboodiri is a professor and associate director for research engagement, for the college of computing, at Wichita State University. He received his PhD at the University of Massachusetts Amherst in 2008, where his primary focus was on mobile computing and wireless network. Dr. Namboodiri has retinitis pigmentosa, a progressive and rare disease that causes vision loss. After some time teaching and researching in mobile technology, he began shifting his focus.

Dr. Namboodiri brought his expertise of wireless technology into the accessibility specialty. He became an adjunct scientist at the Envision Research Institute, in Wichita Kansas. In 2019, he created the Assistive and Accessible Computing  (ACCESS) Research Lab, at Wichita State University. He is now at the forefront of wayfinding navigation, and smart phone based assistive technologies. He, like many other people with disabilities, transitioned to developing technologies related to their disability. I hope to follow his lead intake my experiences into the assistive technology space.
